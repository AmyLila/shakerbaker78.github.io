The Health Benefits of a Cold Plunge Daily

Amy Baker

Ah, wintertime. A time for cozying up and staying warm at all times, right? Well actually, wrong. You should be getting cold at least once daily, for fun and for your health. Though there are many ways to get cold, especially this time of year, a cold plunge is one of the best ways. The definition of a cold plunge is submerging oneself in cold water for at least 7 minutes – though the exact time is really up to the person in question. Though this sounds like something only a crazy person would do, especially during these chilly months, the benefits far outweigh the few minutes of cold.

Today most people want one thing – to be happy. Believe it or not, a daily cold plunge decreases depression symptoms by increasing the release of feel good hormones in the brain. Cold water also helps lessen the inflammation and swelling of sore muscles, so if you don’t want to cold plunge daily, at least do so after a workout. Another benefit to cold water daily is that it increases your metabolism and therefore burns fat. Cold brings about shivering, which fires up the immune system. Though the benefits are worth the cold, a word of warning. Some water is truly too cold to plunge in for long amounts of time. If you’re plunging in the ocean don’t go without a wetsuit if the water is below 50 degrees Fahrenheit, and if you’re swimming in lakes or rivers don’t go if there’s ice.
