How to Enjoy the Outdoors Year Round

Amy Baker

It doesn’t matter what time of year it is, there’s nothing like being outside. But some seasons are harder than others, so how can we truly enjoy the outdoors year-round? First, know the season and what weather conditions accompany is. Are your summers wet or dry? How about winter, is it mild or harsh? That’s step one – know your surroundings. The next step is figuring out how to deal with your surroundings, and that’s what I’m here to help you with. I’ll break it down into 4 different climates – cold and wet, hot and wet, cold and dry, hot and dry.

The biggest thing you need to worry about is how to dress. For cold and wet you want lots of layer and waterproof shoes. Those are the two must haves. You need to keep warm while not getting too sweaty – your downfall comes when that sweat freezes onto you. And if your feet get wet, you’re done. The same clothing with the cold and dry, you can be a little more lenient with the shoes. For hot and wet it’s the opposite – as little clothing as possible and breathable shoes, perhaps even good hiking sandals. The same with hot and dry. I would also recommend wearing a hat and sunglasses for your adventures year-round.
