How to Stay Warm for Your Outdoor Adventures

Amy Baker

I love the outdoors! Hiking, swimming, rock-climbing, and even just taking a walk are adventures I seek daily and adore. But I often find myself slowing down in the winter and not adventuring near as much as I would like. Though there are many reasons, I think the biggest is I don’t know how to explore a winter wonderland as well as I know how to explore other types of terrain and weather. However, I’ve been trying to get outside more this season, and I’ve found some great ways to stay cozy while hiking up mountains and walking through neighborhoods.

My first piece of advice is this: be prepared! I hate cutting a hike short because of a cold nose or chilly toes. Know what weather you’re dealing with, how long your adventure will be, and plan accordingly. This leads into tip #2, wear layers. Sweat is your enemy, but warmth is your friend, so you must strike a balance. Have a lot on while you warm up and cool down but be able to take clothes off mid-adventure; you don’t want that sweat to freeze to your body! My last piece of advice would be this – bring plenty of fuel! Your body has to burn something, so bring small but filling snacks. Cheese, meat, and granola bars work wonderfully! With that, I hope you can go out and have your own great – and toasty – adventure!
